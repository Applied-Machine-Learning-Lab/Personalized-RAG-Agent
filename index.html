About this tutorial

Personalization has become a fundamental capability in modern AI systems, enabling tailored interactions that align with individual user preferences, contexts, and objectives. Recent research has increasingly emphasized Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) frameworks and their evolution into advanced agent-based architectures within personalized settings to enhance user satisfaction.

In this tutorial, we provide a systematic review examining personalization across the three core stages of RAG: Pre-retrieval, Retrieval, and Generation, highlighting personalized adaptations at each stage. Beyond RAG, we explore the transition to Personalized LLM-based Agents, which extend static RAG systems with agentic capabilities. We begin with an introduction to the formulation and integration of personalization at each stage. Next, we conduct a comprehensive review of the relevant literature, recent advancements, benchmark datasets, and evaluation metrics in their respective domains. Finally, to provide deeper insights, we discuss current limitations and outline several future research directions.
